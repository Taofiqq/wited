About Us

Women in Technical Education and Development (WITED) was established in 1987 by Commonwealth Association of Technical Universities and Polytechnics Africa to improve the percentage access of girl child to sciences, Technology Engineering and mathematics and to identify and tackle those factors that inhibits the percentage enrollment in STEM.
Today, globally technical and vocational training have been taken as a powerful tool to create job opportunities/vacancies which improves the livelihood of people.

Our Vision

To bridge the gender gap and improve the participation of girl-child and in science Technology Education for Employment.

Our Mission

Enhance girl-child education in science and technology Advocacy in favour of female gender in skill/technical and vocational Education training (TVET) development Contribution of our qouta to our nations development Striving and partnering for capacity building for girl-child and women education for excellence.
